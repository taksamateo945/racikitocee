Al Capone’s Early And Personal Life

“Al Capone, born as Alphonse Gabriel Capone on January 17, 1899, in Brooklyn, New York, had parents who had immigrated from Italy, Teresa, a seamstress, and Gabriele, a barber.

He had eight siblings.

Capone received his education at a Catholic school but faced expulsion at the age of 14 for striking a teacher.

Following this, he worked at a bowling alley and a candy store and even pursued semi-professional baseball for a couple of years.

Al Capone’s private life was intertwined with his criminal endeavors and his family.

In 1918, he married Mary Josephine Coughlin, commonly known as Mae, and their union endured until his passing.

They had a son named Albert Francis “Sonny” Capone, who chose not to pursue a path similar to his father’s.

Capone’s notoriety and criminal activities have cemented his position in history, resulting in numerous depictions of his life in movies, television series, and literature.”
